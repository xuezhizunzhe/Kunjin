# KunJin Phase 4.1 Independent Financial And Product Review

## Decision

Phase 4.1 may close as an engineering and safety increment and proceed to
Phase 5. It does not close the owner's named-candidate goal and does not make
KunJin a financially usable fund-selection adviser.

Scheme B materially improves honesty and operability. KunJin can now form a
bounded educational research scope, inspect the readiness of explicitly supplied
subjects, run a finite refresh workflow, and distinguish a working flow from
missing evidence. The real acceptance result demonstrates exactly that
distinction: the flow passed, but comparison evidence remained insufficient,
structural comparability could not be tested, and financial usability was not
yet testable.

This is useful to a beginner as a guided preflight and abstention tool. It is not
a complete answer to which fund to choose, how to buy, whether current market
conditions support buying, or whether a held fund should be sold today. A 90%
beginner-help claim remains unsupported.

## Review Basis

This review considered the final Scheme B revision set, the Phase 4.1 design,
the research-scope and readiness implementation, the finite acceptance
orchestration, relevant tests, the prior Phase 4 independent review, both final
review PASS results, and the supplied anonymous engineering and owner acceptance
summaries.

Both final reviews reported PASS. Fresh independent verification of the final
revision produced:

- complete repository regression: 3044 passed;
- Phase 4.1 local acceptance: 385 passed;
- Phase 4.1 fault acceptance: 84 passed; and
- Ruff and acceptance-script Bash syntax checks: passed.

The supplied private acceptance summaries additionally recorded:

- real engineering acceptance: completed with a passing flow and explicit
  degraded evidence states; and
- owner read-only acceptance: completed successfully with authenticated safe
  status metadata and fixed non-authorization boundaries.

The test and review results establish contract quality within the defined
scope. They do not upgrade missing public-product evidence or create a real
owner candidate question.

## Six Independent Acceptance Dimensions

| Dimension | Observed result | Independent interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Engineering flow | `pass` | The finite initial-check, source-status, permitted refresh, terminal-state, and final-check sequence obeyed its operational contract. This is strong engineering evidence only. |
| Evidence readiness | `partial` | At least one usable or authenticated component existed, but the evidence set was incomplete. Partial is not nearly ready and does not imply that the remaining gaps are immaterial. |
| Comparison evidence readiness | `insufficient_data` | The real engineering subjects lacked enough authenticated evidence for a meaningful relative comparison. No comparison conclusion should be inferred. |
| Structural comparability | `not_testable` | The run did not establish that the subjects were comparable or structurally different. It does not mean similar, diversified, safe, or suitable. |
| Financial usability | `not_yet_testable` | There was no intentional real-owner candidate set on which to test a personal financial decision. Engineering success cannot answer this question. |
| Candidate-code discovery | `not_implemented` | The beginner still must intentionally supply or uniquely confirm candidates. Research scope does not turn a category choice into a purchase list. |

`held_binding_observed=true` proves that the system correctly recognized at
least one current-position relationship in the engineering flow. It does not
prove holdings completeness, candidate merit, suitability, diversification, or
an action conclusion.

The engineering subjects exist solely to test integration boundaries. They are
not candidates, recommendations, or evidence of owner preference. Their
financial interpretation remains prohibited.

## Owner State

The read-only owner acceptance repaired the earlier false technical degradation
and produced current safe metadata:

- Phase A: `confirmed` and `fresh`;
- Phase B: `constrained` and `fresh`, with no blocking code and the retained
  `monthly_ceiling_constrained` constraint;
- Phase C: `range_available` and `fresh`, with the retained
  `horizon_binding` and `monthly_ceiling_constrained` constraints; and
- `action_authorized=false`, `exact_amount_available=false`, and
  `automatic_trade=false`.

This is a real improvement over the earlier transient gate result: the system
can now read and report the owner's authenticated safe states without exposing
private financial values. It is not purchase approval. `constrained` remains
`research_only`; `range_available` is an abstract boundary, not a target,
product choice, contribution plan, or transaction amount.

The owner's 10 current positions are a small learning portfolio. They do not
establish long-term preference, purchase intent, target allocation, risk
appetite, or future candidate identity. Automatically turning those positions
into a candidate set would be a category error and would invalidate the product
claim.

## What Phase 4.1 Improves

- It gives a beginner a closed educational vocabulary for objective, horizon,
  and product category without pretending that the selected category is
  personally suitable.
- It makes the gap between having a research question and having candidate
  identities explicit.
- It projects public-product, portfolio-binding, and personal-gate readiness
  separately instead of collapsing them into one score.
- It returns bounded existing refresh or manual-supplementation paths and stops
  after one finite orchestration, avoiding retries, polling, or adapter work.
- It fixes the owner acceptance path so current encrypted personal-state
  metadata can be observed read-only instead of being misreported as transient.
- It preserves non-authorization even when personal gates are current and
  non-blocked.

The most important improvement is epistemic: the system can now say whether the
workflow worked, whether some evidence exists, whether comparison is ready, and
whether financial use was actually tested without treating those statements as
synonyms.

## What Phase 4.1 Does Not Improve

- It does not discover candidate codes or resolve the beginner's initial choice
  set.
- It does not make the real engineering subjects comparison-ready; the observed
  comparison evidence remained `insufficient_data`.
- It does not prove structural comparability, because the observed result was
  `not_testable`.
- It does not establish financial usability, because no intentional owner
  candidate set existed.
- It does not add current valuation, market-regime, authenticated market
  direction, expected-return, or purchase-timing evidence.
- It does not complete holdings look-through, correlation, stress testing,
  transaction-cost, channel, settlement, or exact-output authorization.
- It does not improve thesis interpretation, event monitoring, redemption
  evidence, or sell timing. A fresh personal gate is neither a hold signal nor a
  sell signal.

The research-scope taxonomy may still create anchoring risk for a beginner: a
chosen product category can feel like a recommendation even though it is only a
study label. User-facing explanations must continue to put the missing candidate
and product-evidence steps before any fund comparison language.

## Beginner Usefulness

The following 0-100 figures are cautious judgment bands rather than scientific
measurements. They describe current observed help, not ideal capability under
complete future evidence.

| Owner goal | Current usefulness | Independent assessment |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| News and industry understanding | about 50/100 | Phase 4.1 adds research framing, not new news breadth or authenticated industry coverage. Existing partial public intelligence remains the limiting evidence. |
| Market direction and how to buy | about 15/100 | No market-direction engine, valuation conclusion, mature buy gate, channel check, or transaction amount was added. |
| Named candidates, holdings context, and personal guidance | about 40/100 | Research scope, readiness, bounded refresh, and authentic personal-gate metadata are useful advances. Candidate discovery is absent, real comparison evidence is insufficient, and financial usability is untested. |
| Daily question: can I sell? | about 10/100 | Phase 4.1 adds no monitoring or sell-timing evidence and must not convert personal constraints into a sell signal. |
| Whole-portfolio diagnosis | about 55/100 | Held binding is now observed in the engineering flow, but that is narrower than complete portfolio evidence. Existing relationship and disclosed-holdings limits remain. |

Overall beginner usefulness is still roughly in the mid-30s to low-40s out of
100, with most value coming from safe organization, current personal-gate
metadata, and explicit abstention. The system is more coherent, but not
financially complete. Neither the overall product nor the risk-increasing and
sell workflows approach 90%.

## Phase 5 Entry Decision

Proceed to Phase 5, with a narrow holding, reduction, and exit-review scope.
Phase 4.1 has supplied the prerequisite machinery to inspect readiness honestly,
and both reviews and the verification set support technical closure.

Phase 5 must begin from the owner's intentionally selected held fund and a
clearly routed action. It must not treat all 10 learning positions as preferred
assets or convert engineering subjects into candidates. It should preserve the
current Phase B/C constraints, but a constraint alone must never become a sell
signal. Reduction and exit research require current product identity, position,
fee, settlement, event, and thesis evidence, while holding conclusions require
their own complete route and opposing evidence.

Phase 5 acceptance should continue the six-way separation used here. A passing
flow is necessary, but financial usability should pass only on an intentional
owner question with sufficiently current evidence. Exact amounts, automatic
trading, and mature sell timing remain unavailable until their independent
contracts are implemented and accepted.

only anonymous states, stable public constraint labels, aggregate test counts,
and non-authorization conclusions.
